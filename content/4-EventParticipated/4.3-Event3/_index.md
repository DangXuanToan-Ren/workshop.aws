# SUMMARY REPORT: EVENT 3 - CAREER DEVELOPMENT & CORPORATE CULTURE


## I. Event Overview

The third event brought a comprehensive perspective on real career development paths for IT students. The content spanned from building professional foundations through the AWS community, understanding the real nature of work and culture at multinational corporations (MNCs), to debunking common misconceptions about DevOps engineers.

- Kien and Tho: Summary content (waiting for another PowerPoint file).

- Brother Dat & Brother Cuong: "Real stories to corporate culture at multinational corporations".
- Brother Hoang Trong: "What does a DevOps Engineer really do?".


## III. Key Highlights

### 1. Building a career with the AWS ecosystem (Brother Hieu Nghi)
- 8-step development path: Starting from student curiosity -> Join First Cloud Journey -> Workshop & Community activities -> Hands-on Labs -> Build school projects -> Create Portfolio -> Become AWS Partner -> Share knowledge back (Share Back).
- Leveraging the community: Joining groups like AWS Student Builder Group and AWS Community Builders not only helps expand connections but also brings many practical benefits like guidance, swag points and AWS exam vouchers.


### 2. Reality of Data field and MNC Culture (Brother Dat & Brother Cuong)
- Real work of Data Analytics Engineer: Not just stopping at creating reports, but the core is analyzing to find root causes, designing management dashboards, optimizing costs and supporting digital transformation initiatives of the enterprise.
- 4 survival skills: Critical thinking, Communication skills, Data Storytelling and Problem solving.
- MNC recruitment process: Has 4 main barriers: ATS/Screening -> Competency test (Logic/Algorithm) -> Technical interview (using STAR method) -> Culture fit and Leadership assessment.
- Distinctive corporate culture: Emphasizes "No-Blame Post-Mortem" (not blaming, focus on fixing systems) and "Caring & Inclusive" (respecting diversity, people-centric).
- Competency development model (5 levels): From Follower (Executor) -> Learner (Active learner) -> Problem Solver (Problem solver) -> System Thinker (System thinker) -> Super Star (Leader). At the same time, apply the "Right Job" philosophy in all 3 roles: Being a Person, Being a Professional and Being a Citizen.


### 3. Debunking DevOps in practice (Brother Hoang Trong)
- Market picture: DevOps engineers along with AI/ML and Data Engineer are the positions with the strongest demand and salary growth in Vietnam during 2016-2025.
- Breaking misconceptions: Contrary to the thought that DevOps is just someone who "writes CI/CD pipelines" or "fixes issues at midnight", the actual scope of work depends heavily on company size, project complexity and team structure culture.
- Learning strategy: Should not chase after flashy tools from the beginning. Start with solid foundations: Linux, basic Networking, programming language (Python/Golang), Git, CI/CD and Containers.
- "Hard-earned" lessons: Copying commands from the internet doesn't help you understand the essence. Always ask "Why?" before asking "How?", learn to identify the right owner of the problem, focus on communication and absolutely don't be a "hero" single-handedly rescuing the system. Tools change, but thinking should not. Use AI to optimize efficiency, but never "turn off" your own thinking.


## IV. Key Values Gained

- About learning mindset: System foundations (Linux, Networking, Programming) are more important than knowing how to use many flashy tools.
- About direction: Advancement isn't about titles, but about the shift from just receiving tasks (Follower) to being able to see problems at a system-wide scale (System Thinker).
- About culture perspective: Understanding the recruitment process picture and core values (No-Blame, Inclusive) of multinational corporations helps position a clear work attitude for candidates.


## V. Plan to Apply in Practice

- Rebuild DevOps learning direction: Focus on completing Linux and Networking knowledge before diving into Kubernetes or complex CI/CD processes.
- Practice STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) when describing projects (like SEVEN Charging or restaurant management system) in CV and interviews to be ready for technical assessments at large enterprises.
- Participate more deeply in communities like AWS Student Builder Group to seek hands-on opportunities and take AWS certifications, completing the "8-step" path.


## VI. Personal Reflections

The event brought a lot of "real" things very strongly. Sharing from Brother Trong helped me clarify that I need to focus on foundational knowledge rather than just learning new tools. At the same time, the competency development model and corporate culture analysis from Brother Dat and Brother Cuong gave me a more standard perspective on the professional work environment I'm heading towards in the upcoming internship.
